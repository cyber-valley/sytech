alias:: saga rambat, rosary pea, jequirity bean
tags:: species

- ![image.png](../assets/image_1715010968304_0.png){:height 430, :width 628}
- native: tropical and subtropical regions
- highly toxic plant, particularly its seeds, which contain the poison abrin, type 2 ribosome-inactivating protein (RIP) that inhibits protein synthesis, leading to cell death. [[seeds]] are more poisonous than [[ricinus communis]]
- wiki:: ![abrus_precatorius.pdf](../assets/abrus_precatorius_1719126734571_0.pdf)
- products::
	- **fruit**: the seeds of the fruit are highly toxic and should not be consumed
- uses::
	- **plants/fruits**: toxic, should not be consumed
	- **plants/medicine**: parts of the plant are used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties, but extreme caution is advised due to toxicity
		- **the leaves** (decoctions and infusions): treat various diseases, such as respiratory and gastrointestinal diseases
		- **bark**: relieve pain in joints and muscles, as well as to treat headaches and fevers
		- **the seeds** (despite their toxicity): treat toothaches and other ailments
	- **other parts of the plant**:
		- **flowers**: to prepare infusions and decoctions, which can help with skin diseases and urinary tract problems
		- **the young shoots and fruits**: to strengthen the immune system and increase overall body tone
		- **the roots**: as an analgesic and anti-inflammatory agent, as well as to improve digestion and eliminate toxins from the body
